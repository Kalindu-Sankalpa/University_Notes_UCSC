# learning Objectives

1. Fundamental Project Objectives
	The primary aim of software project management is to ensure that products are **delivered on time, within budget and schedule constraints, and satisfy the client's requirements**. Because software projects are often complex and non-tangible, a major goal is to provide a framework to **manage this complexity** through effective planning and control.
	
2. Organizational and Systems Understanding
	Students are expected to master how projects interact with the broader environment, specifically:
	-  **Systems Thinking:** Describing a "systems view" of project management and applying it to IT contexts.
	- **Organizational Context:** Understanding how organizational frames, structures, and culture (values and behaviors) impact project success.
	- **Stakeholder Management:** Explaining why the commitment of top management and the management of stakeholder relationships are critical.
	- **Life Cycles:** Distinguishing between project and product life cycles and understanding the specific phases of each

3. Process Groups and Methodologies
	he course aims to provide a deep dive into the "how-to" of management:
	- **The Five Process Groups:** Describing and applying the five project management process groups: **initiating, planning, executing, monitoring and controlling, and closing**.
	- **Knowledge Areas:** Relating these process groups to the ten project management knowledge areas, such as scope, schedule, cost, and risk management.
	- **Methodologies:** Discussing how organizations develop specific methodologies (like PMBOK, PRINCE2, or Agile) to meet their needs.
	- **Agile vs. Predictive:** Comparing traditional "Waterfall" approaches with Agile focuses, including the ability to describe the differences in their execution and results.

4. Financial and Technical Skills
	significant portion of the course focuses on **Capital Budgeting** to determine project viability. Students learn to:
	- Evaluate the **Time Value of Money** and understand concepts like **Opportunity Cost** and Discounted Cash Flow.
	- Calculate and apply financial techniques to choose between projects, including **Net Present Value (NPV), Internal Rate of Return (IRR), Profitability Index (PI), and Payback Period**.

5. Professional Skill Development
	Beyond technical knowledge, the course emphasizes the cultivation of a specific management spectrum, focusing on the **"Four P’s"**: People, Problem, Process, and Project. Key professional skills targeted include **leadership, communication, problem-solving, negotiating, and mentoring**.
