**Software Project Management (SPM)** is a discipline aimed at ensuring that software is delivered **on time, within budget and schedule constraints, and satisfies the requirements of the client**. Unlike general projects, software projects are uniquely challenging because software is **intangible (invisible)**, making progress difficult to see; it is often more **complex** than other engineering artifacts; and it is highly **flexible**, meaning it is frequently subjected to changing organizational requirements.

![[University Of Colombo - School Of Computing/Semester - 02/IS1209 - Information Technology Project Management/Images/img_01.svg]]

The core of effective project management focuses on the **Management Spectrum**, often referred to as the **Four P’s**:

1. **People:** Cultivating motivated, skilled teams and managing stakeholders.

2. **Product (or Problem):** Establishing objectives and scope before planning begins.

3. **Process:** Providing a framework and plan for development.

4. **Project:** Planning and controlling the endeavor to manage complexity.

Every project operates under the **Triple Constraint**, which requires a balance between
- **Time**
- **Cost**
- **Quality (Scope)**

To manage these, projects are divided into a **Project Life Cycle**, typically consisting of phases like 
- **Initiation**
- **Planning**
- **Execution**
- **Closure**

Within these phases, managers perform five key **Process Groups**: initiating, planning, executing, monitoring and controlling, and closing.

Success often depends on **systems thinking**—understanding how the project fits into the broader organization—and securing **top management commitment** to provide resources and leadership.

# Summary

- **Project Definition:** A temporary endeavor undertaken to create a unique product or service.

- **Characteristics:** Non-routine, specific objectives, predetermined time span, and utilizes constrained resources.

- **The 4 P’s of Management:** People, Product, Process, and Project.

- **The Triple Constraint:** Time, Cost, and Quality/Scope. Changing one usually affects the others.

- **Why Software is Different:**
	1. **Invisibility:** Hard to measure physical progress.
	2. **Complexity:** Higher than other engineering products of similar value.
	3. **Rapid Evolution:** Technology changes quickly.

- **5 Project Management Process Groups:**
    1. **Initiating:** Defining scope and authorizing the project.
    2. **Planning:** Devising a workable scheme to meet organizational needs.
    3. **Executing:** Coordinating people and resources.
    4. **Monitoring & Controlling:** Measuring progress and taking corrective action.
    5. **Closing:** Formalizing acceptance and ending the project efficiently.

- **Stakeholders:** Can be internal (team, sponsor) or external (customers, contractors).

- **Key Financial Metrics (Capital Budgeting):** Used to decide project viability. Includes **Net Present Value (NPV)**, **Internal Rate of Return (IRR)**, and **Payback Period**.
