>[!tip]
>Modern quality management emphasizes that **quality is defined by the customer**, prefers **prevention over inspection**, and recognizes that management is responsible for the majority (85%) of quality costs.

# 1. The Three Main Processes

- **Plan Quality:**
  Identifying which quality standards are relevant and how to satisfy them. Key outputs include a **Quality Management Plan** and quality metrics.
- **Perform Quality Assurance:**
  Evaluating overall project performance on a regular basis to ensure the project will satisfy relevant quality standards. It focuses on **continuous improvement** and utilizes tools like **quality audits** and benchmarking.
- **Perform Quality Control:**
  Monitoring specific project results to ensure they comply with quality standards. This process identifies ways to eliminate causes of unsatisfactory performance.

# 2. The Cost of Quality

Quality is managed by balancing the **Cost of Conformance** against the **Cost of Nonconformance**:

- **Conformance:**
  Includes **prevention costs** (training, planning) and **appraisal costs** (testing, inspections).
- **Nonconformance:**
  Includes **internal failure costs** (rework, scrap before delivery) and **external failure costs** (warranty, lawsuits after delivery).

# 3. Key Tools for Analysis

The PPT outlines several technical tools for controlling quality:

- **Cause-and-Effect Diagrams:**
  Also known as **Fishbone or Ishikawa diagrams**, these help find the root cause of problems.
- **Control Charts:**
  Graphic displays of data over time used to determine if a process is "in control." The **Seven Run Rule** states that if seven consecutive points are on one side of the mean, the process should be examined.
- **Pareto Charts:**
  A histogram that helps identify the "vital few" problems. It follows the **80/20 rule**: 80% of problems are often caused by 20% of the causes.
- **Six Sigma:**
  A methodology striving for perfection (no more than **3.4 defects per million opportunities**) using the **DMAIC** process: Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, and Control.

# 4. Testing in IT Projects

Unlike general manufacturing, IT quality relies heavily on software testing throughout the life cycle:

- **Unit Testing:** Testing individual components.
- **Integration Testing:** Testing functionally grouped components.
- **System Testing:** Testing the entire system as one entity.
- **User Acceptance Testing (UAT):** Independent testing by the end user before formal acceptance.

# Short Note for Study

## Topic: Project Quality Management

- **Definition:**
  The degree to which inherent characteristics fulfill requirements (ISO 9000).
- **Core Concepts:**
	- **Conformance to requirements:** Meeting written specifications.
	- **Fitness for use:** The product can be used as intended.
- **The 3 Processes:**
  Plan Quality, Quality Assurance (QA), Quality Control (QC).
- **Gold Plating:**
  Giving the customer extras that do not add value; this is generally discouraged.
- **Seven Run Rule:**
  A tool in control charts used to identify non-random patterns requiring investigation.
- **DMAIC:**
  The Six Sigma improvement cycle (Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control).
- **Maturity Models:**
  Frameworks like **CMMI** (Capability Maturity Model Integration) help organizations improve their processes through five levels, from "Incomplete" to "Optimizing".
- **Management Responsibility:**
  Deming suggests that management is responsible for 85% of quality costs.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Analogy:** Project Quality Management is like **maintaining a five-star restaurant**. **Planning Quality** is writing the recipes and training the chefs. **Quality Assurance** is the head chef walking through the kitchen to make sure everyone is following the recipes and using fresh ingredients. **Quality Control** is tasting the soup before it goes to the table to ensure it isn't too salty. The **Cost of Conformance** is the money spent on high-quality ingredients and training, while the **Cost of Nonconformance** is the cost of a customer sending a meal back or leaving a bad review.
