#Lesson-Plan 

>[!Goals]
>The primary goal of the IS1212 Probability and Statistics course is to introduce students to the science of **collecting, organizing, analyzing, and presenting data** to draw conclusions in the best possible way. The course aims to provide a sufficient background in statistical techniques so that students can **formulate real-world problems** in statistical terms and make informed decisions about populations while accounting for individual variability.
>
>Additionally, the course focuses on converting **raw facts into useful information** through both numerical and graphical summaries. By mastering these concepts, students will be able to interpret results, evaluate published data, and **generalize decisions** across various disciplines, including computer science, marketing, and medicine.

---
# Learning Objectives

- **Evaluating published data** and interpreting statistical results.

- **Collecting and summarizing data** meaningfully to bring out its main features.

- **Presenting findings** effectively through reports, verbal communication, and graphical tools like charts, plots, and tables.

- **Formulating and solving problems** using appropriate statistical methods.

- **Understanding measures of location and dispersion**, such as mean, median, mode, variance, and standard deviation.

- **Grasping the fundamentals of probability** and both discrete and continuous probability distributions.

- **Distinguishing between different types of variables** (qualitative vs. quantitative) and levels of measurement.

- **Applying sampling techniques** to take representative subsets from a population for analysis.

- **Drawing inferences** and generalizing from a sample to a broader population.

To think about these goals, imagine statistics as a **translator**: it takes a messy, unorganized language of raw numbers and translates it into a clear, visual story that helps people make the right choices.
