> [!TIP] Objectives
> The primary objective of this module, **"IS1206 - Mathematics for Computing,"** is to provide students with the foundational mathematical tools necessary for computer science, data science, and algorithm analysis.

The following are the analyzed goals and learning outcomes categorized by the core topics found in your sources:

1. Mastering Arithmetic and Number Properties

A fundamental goal is for students to **apply arithmetic operations and number properties** to solve computational problems. This includes:

- Understanding the **Real Number System**, including rational, irrational, and prime numbers.
- Mastering the **order of operations** (BODMAS) and the properties of signed numbers.
- Calculating the **Highest Common Factor (HCF)** and **Least Common Multiple (LCM)**, which are essential for understanding divisibility and efficiency in algorithms.

2. Modeling with Set Theory

The curriculum aims to teach students how to **use set theory and Venn diagrams to model and analyze data relationships**. Key objectives include:

- Defining and identifying various types of sets (finite, infinite, power sets, and universal sets).
- Performing **set operations** such as union, intersection, complement, and symmetric difference.
- Understanding **relations and functions**, specifically focusing on their properties, domains, ranges, and compositions.

3. Developing Logical Reasoning

The lesson intends for students to **apply basic principles of logic** to construct and evaluate logical expressions, which forms the basis of programming and system design. Objectives include:

- Using **propositional logic** (truth tables, connectives, and tautologies) to remove ambiguity in communication and specifications.
- Applying **predicate logic and quantifiers** (Universal and Existential) to express complex mathematical and system-level statements.
- Translating natural language (English) into **unambiguous formal specifications** for hardware and software systems.

4. Combinatorics and Counting Techniques

Students must learn to **solve problems using advanced counting techniques**, which are vital for algorithm analysis and probability. Specific goals involve:

- Distinguishing between and applying **permutations and combinations** based on the problem context.
- Utilizing the **pigeonhole principle**, the **binomial theorem**, and the **rules of sum and product** to solve combinatorial problems.
- Developing strategies for **distributing objects into boxes**, whether they are distinguishable or indistinguishable.

5. Linear Algebra: Vectors and Matrices

A major objective is for students to **perform vector and matrix operations** to solve systems of linear equations and represent data. This includes:

- Representing systems of linear equations using **matrices and augmented matrices**.
- Performing matrix algebra (addition, scalar multiplication, and matrix multiplication) and finding **inverses and transposes**.
- Utilizing **Gaussian Elimination** and **Reduced Row Echelon Form (RREF)** to systematically solve linear systems.

6. Solving Equations with Indices and Logarithms

Finally, the lesson aims to equip students with the ability to **solve exponential and logarithmic equations**. Students are expected to:

- Apply the **laws of indices and logarithms** to simplify complex expressions.
- Understand the relationship between **index form and logarithmic form** to solve for unknown variables.

# Course Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of this course, students should be able to:
- Apply arithmetic operations and number properties to solve computational problems.
- Model and analyze data relationships using set theory and Venn diagrams.
- Construct and evaluate logical expressions using principles of mathematical logic.
- Solve problems using indices, logarithms, and counting techniques such as permutations and combinations.
- Perform vector and matrix operations to solve linear equations and represent data.

# Topics Covered

The comprehensive list of covered topics for the **IS1206 - Mathematics for Computing** course, structured according to the syllabus :

1. [[Numbers and Arithmetic]]
	- **Number Systems:** Understanding the properties of Natural numbers, Whole numbers, Integers, Rational numbers, Irrational numbers, and Real numbers.
	- **Arithmetic Operations:** Mastering addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division of real numbers and signed numbers.
	- **Order of Operations:** Applying the **BODMAS rule** (Brackets, Order, Division, Multiplication, Addition, Subtraction) to complex expressions.
	- **Prime and Composite Numbers:** Identifying prime numbers, determining prime factors using factor trees, and recognizing composite numbers.
	- **Factors and Multiples:** Calculating the **Highest Common Factor (HCF/GCD)** and **Least Common Multiple (LCM)** using listing, prime factorization, and division methods.
	- **Fractions:** Classifying types of fractions and performing reduction, multiplication, addition, division, and subtraction.
	- **Divisibility Rules:** Utilizing shortcuts to check divisibility for numbers 1 through 13.

2. [[Set Theory]]
	- **Basic Concepts:** Definitions of sets, elements, and cardinality.
	- **Set Representation:** Using **Roster form** (listing elements) and **Set-Builder form** (defining properties).
	- **Types of Sets:** Differentiating between Singleton, Empty, Finite, Infinite, Equal, Equivalent, Subsets (and Proper Subsets), Power sets, and Universal sets.
	- **Set Operations:** Performing Union, Intersection, Complement, Set Difference, and Symmetric Difference.
	- **Venn Diagrams:** Diagrammatic representation of sets and their relationships to solve practical involvement problems.

3. [[Mathematical Logic]]
	- **Propositional Logic:** Identifying propositions, determining truth values, and using logical connectives (NOT, AND, OR, XOR, Implication, Bi-conditional).
	- **Truth Tables and Precedence:** Constructing truth tables for compound propositions and following operator precedence rules.
	- **Translations and Specifications:** Translating English sentences into logical expressions and checking the **consistency** of system specifications.
	- **Equivalences and Tautologies:** Recognizing Tautologies, Contradictions, and using **De Morgan’s Laws** to establish logical equivalences.
	- **Predicate Logic:** Understanding Predicates and using **Universal (∀) and Existential (∃) Quantifiers**.

4. [[Indices and Logarithms]]
	- **Indices:** Applying the **Laws of Indices** and solving exponential equations by equating bases and exponents.
	- **Roots and Surds:** Simplifying surds, understanding properties of roots, and **rationalizing denominators**.
	- **Logarithms:** Converting between index and logarithmic form, utilizing **Laws of Logarithms**, the **Change of Base formula**, and understanding Common vs. Natural logarithms.

5. [[Techniques of Counting]]
	- **Fundamental Principles:** Applying the **Product Rule** (sequential tasks) and **Sum Rule** (non-simultaneous tasks).
	- **Advanced Principles:** Utilizing the **Subtraction Rule (Inclusion-Exclusion)** for overlapping sets, the **Division Rule**, and **Tree Diagrams** for visualizing outcomes.
	- **Pigeonhole Principle:** Applying both the basic and **Generalized Pigeonhole Principle** to guarantee shared outcomes.
	- **Permutations and Combinations:** Calculating ordered arrangements (permutations) and unordered selections (combinations), including cases with **repetition** and **indistinguishable objects**.
	- **Distribution:** Techniques for distributing objects (distinguishable or indistinguishable) into boxes.

6. [[Linear Algebra - Vectors and Matrices]]
	- **Linear Equations:** Solving single linear equations and systems of linear equations through graphical and algebraic methods.
	- **Matrix Basics:** Notation, dimensions, and identifying special types like Square, Diagonal, Identity, Triangular, Symmetric, and Zero matrices.
	- **Matrix Algebra:** Performing equality checks, scalar multiplication, addition, subtraction, and matrix multiplication.
	- **Vectors:** Understanding magnitude and direction, performing vector addition and scalar multiplication, and calculating the **dot (inner) product**.
	- **Advanced Matrix Operations:** Finding the **Transpose** and **Inverse** of a matrix.
	- **Systems Solutions:** Representing systems with **Augmented Matrices** and solving them using **Gaussian Elimination** and **Reduced Row Echelon Form (RREF)**.

7. [[Relations and Functions]]
	- **Cartesian Products:** Defining the set of all ordered pairs between two sets.
	- **Relations:** Representing connections via Roster form, Set-Builder form, Arrow diagrams, and Tables.
	- **Functions:** Defining functions where every input has a unique output, and identifying the **Domain, Co-domain, and Range**.
	- **Function Composition:** Defining **composite functions (f ∘ g)** and understanding properties like associativity and identity.
