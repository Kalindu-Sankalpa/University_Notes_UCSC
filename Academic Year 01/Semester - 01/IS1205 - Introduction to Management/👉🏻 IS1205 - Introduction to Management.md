> [!TIP] Objectives
> The primary objective of this course, **IS1205 - Introduction to Management**, is to provide you with a **sound understanding of management theories, practices, and applications** to enhance your managerial decision-making abilities.

Here is an analysis of the specific goals and objectives for this lesson:

1. Core Educational Objectives
	 The lesson is designed to prepare students with the necessary skills to work in **managerial capacities** across any type of organization. To achieve this, the course aims to familiarize you with:
	 
	 - **Management and Marketing principles**.
	 - **Motivation and Leadership techniques**.
	 - **Business Information Systems and eBusiness applications**.
	 - **Financial Accounting and Teamwork**.

2. Functional Management Goals
	A key goal is to understand management as a four-part process used to optimize organizational resources:
	
	- **Planning:** Establishing goals and courses of action.
	- **Organizing:** Structuring people and resources to work together.
	- **Leading:** Directing and influencing task-related activities.
	- **Controlling:** Ensuring actual activities align with planned ones.

3. Strategic Analysis Goals
	The sources emphasize that students should be able to analyze a business's position within its environment. Specific learning goals include:
	
	- **Developing Business and Marketing Strategies:** Learning how to create a strategic path for a company.
	- **Environmental Analysis:** Mastering tools like **PEST Analysis** (Political, Economical, Social, Technological), **SWOT Analysis** (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats), and **Porter’s Five Forces**.
	- **Market Research:** Understanding how to systematically collect information to increase marketing activities and understand competitor behavior.

4. Ethical and Social Awareness
	The lesson sets a high priority on **Ethics and Professionalism**. The goals here are:
	
	- To practice making **principled choices** between right and wrong.
	- To understand the "10 Commandments of Computer Ethics".
	- To recognize the evolution of society from the Industrial age to the **Knowledge and AI societies**, where knowledge is the basic economic resource.

5. Assessment and Practical Application
	Finally, the lesson has clear practical objectives tied to your grades:
	
	- **Project Work (40%):** You are expected to select a company, study its industry sector, analyze its competitors, and develop a formal business report and presentation
	- **Final Exam (60%):** This will test your ability to apply these concepts to a **case study**.

# Topics Covered

The course **IS1205 - Introduction to Management** covers a wide range of topics designed to build a foundation in management principles and their application in modern business.

Here are the primary topics covered in the lesson:

1. [[Introduction to Management Concepts]]
	
	- **Definition of Management:** Understanding it as the process of planning, organizing, leading, and controlling to optimize resources.
	- **Efficiency vs. Effectiveness:** Learning the difference between "doing things right" and "doing the right thing".
	- **The Management Process:** The four functional areas: **Planning** (establishing goals), **Organizing** (structuring people and resources), **Leading** (directing and influencing), and **Controlling** (aligning activities with plans).
	- **Managerial Levels & Roles:** Differentiating between top, middle, and first-line managers. Exploring **interpersonal, informational, and decisional roles**.
	- **Managerial Skills:** Developing **conceptual, human, and technical skills**.
	- **Emotional Intelligence:** Understanding its five facets: self-awareness, self-regulation, motivation, empathy, and social skills.

2. [[Business Organization & Environment]]

	- **Social Transformation:** The evolution of society from the Agricultural and Industrial eras to the Information, Knowledge, and **AI societies**.
	- **Division of Labour:** Concepts from Adam Smith, Henry Ford (assembly lines), and Alfred Sloan (management structures).
	- **Internal and External Environment:** Identifying internal stakeholders (employees, board of directors) and external factors (customers, government, competitors).
	- **Strategic Analysis Tools:**
		- **PEST Analysis:** Political, Economical, Social, and Technological variables.
		- **SWOT Analysis:** Evaluating Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats.
		- **Porter’s Five Forces:** Analyzing competitive rivalry, bargaining power of suppliers/buyers, and the threat of new entrants or substitutes.

3. [[Business & Marketing Strategies]]

	- **Developing Strategy:** Creating paths for survival, sustainability, and growth.
	- **Strategic Positioning:** Choosing between being a cost leader or using differentiation.
	- **Marketing Management:** Defining marketing and understanding demand states (negative, latent, full, etc.).
	- **Market Research:** The process of collecting information to understand competitors and changing consumer needs.
	- **The Marketing Mix:** Applying the **4 Ps** (Product, Price, Place, Promotion) for goods and the **7 Ps** (adding People, Processes, Physical Evidence) for services.
	- **Product Life Cycle:** Managing products through Introduction, Growth, Maturity, and Decline stages.
	- **The Dell Direct Model:** A case study on direct customer relationships and build-to-order efficiency.

4. [[Ethics and Professional Issues]]

	- **Defining Ethics:** Making principled choices between right and wrong.
	- **Decision-Making Tests:** Using informal guidelines like the **Mom Test, TV Test, and Smell Test** to evaluate actions.
	- **Computer Ethics:** Adhering to the "10 Commandments of Computer Ethics".
	- **Workplace Issues:** Addressing data collection, security, privacy, and computer crimes.

5. [[Motivation and Leadership]]

	- **Motivation Theories:**
	    - **Content Theories:** Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs, ERG Theory, Herzberg’s Two-Factor Theory, and McClelland’s Learned Needs.
	    - **Process Theories:** Expectancy, Equity, and Goal-Setting theories.
	    - **Reinforcement Theory:** How consequences influence future behavior.
	- **Leadership Styles:**
	    - **Path-Goal Theory:** Directive, supportive, participative, and achievement-oriented styles.
	    - **Transactional vs. Transformational Leadership:** Shifting from reward-based management to visionary, inspiring leadership.

6. [[Information Systems and eBusiness]]

	- **Business Information Systems (BIS):** The use of technology in Supply Chain Management (SCM), Knowledge Management (KM), and Customer Relationship Management (CRM).
	- **IT in Marketing:** Advantages like global communication, interactivity, and personalization.
	- **eBusiness Applications:** Digitization across industries (e.g., web banking) and the rise of eMarketplaces and auctions.
