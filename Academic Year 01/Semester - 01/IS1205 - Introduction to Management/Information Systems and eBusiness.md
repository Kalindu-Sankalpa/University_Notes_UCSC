# 1. Business Information Systems (BIS) Overview

In modern management, Information Systems are integrated across the **traditional value chain** to optimize the flow of information between suppliers, manufacturers, and customers. Key components include:

- **SCM (Supply Chain Management):** Systems that link the supplier and the manufacturer to manage the supply channel.
- **CRM (Customer Relationship Management):** Tools used to manage interactions with resellers and end customers to build loyalty.
- **Functional Systems:** Various technical systems support different organizational levels, including **TPS** (Transaction Processing Systems), **MIS** (Management Information Systems), **ERP** (Enterprise Resource Planning), and **DSS** (Decision Support Systems).
- **KM (Knowledge Management):** Specialized systems designed to capture, store, and utilize the organization's intellectual capital.

# 2. Business Models: Old vs. Online

The transition to a digital environment has fundamentally changed how businesses operate:

- **Physical Model (Traditional):** Relies on physical operations, critical physical locations, and traditional distribution or retail channels. Barriers to entry include high costs and established physical relationships.
- **Online Model (Digital):** Focuses on **online operations** and collaborative competencies. It allows for **global coverage** regardless of physical location and uses "e-intermediaries" for information and service.
- **Strategic Shift:** In the online model, **IT is a strategy**, not just a function. Entry barriers are based on brand name and 1-to-1 relationships, and switching costs for customers are often lower.

# 3. IT and Marketing

Using Information Technology provides several critical advantages for marketing strategies:

- **Speed and Global Communication:** News and information travel faster than ever, allowing for immediate worldwide reach.
- **Wider Access:** Organizations can reach broader demographics through digital platforms.
- **Interactivity and Multimedia:** Engaging customers through varied digital content types.
- **Personalization:** Tailoring information and services to the specific needs of individual users.

# 4. Digital Transformation: Atoms to Bits

Many industries have experienced **product or service disruption** as traditional physical formats (atoms) are replaced by digital formats (bits):

- **Recorded Music:** From LP records and tapes to CDs and MP3s.
- **Journalism:** From physical newspapers to websites, digital magazines, and social media.
- **Banking:** Shifting from cash and checks to **smart cards** and web-based payment systems.
- **Photo-finishing:** Moving from film-based paper photos to purely digital storage and film-to-digital services.

# 5. eBusiness Applications and Disruption

The internet has introduced entirely new ways of conducting business:

- **Market Makers (eMarketplaces):** Online auctions like **eBay** put the buyer in control and establish "pure market prices" through massive C2C (consumer-to-consumer) activity.
- **E-Branding and Extensions:** Companies like Priceline.com leveraged digital platforms to disrupt diverse sectors including airline tickets, groceries, and home mortgages.
- **Price Disruption:** Consumers now use **intelligent agents** (e.g., PriceSCAN) to instantly find the lowest prices across the web.
- **New Pricing Models:** Some businesses earn profit primarily through **advertisements** while offering products at lower-than-cost prices, or use "buy-cycle" schedules where prices drop as more buyers join a group.
