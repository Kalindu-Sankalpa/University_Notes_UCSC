#Lesson-Plan 


> [!TIP] Objectives
> The primary goal is to provide participants with knowledge on **basic concepts of information systems, components, functions, and roles in organizations**, as well as the major concerns for acquiring IS and the potential issues they can create.

# Core Learning Outcomes

Upon completing this lesson, students are expected to achieve several high-level competencies:

- **Requirements Identification:** Learn to identify specific information system requirements for various business needs.
- **System Analysis:** Identify components, characteristics, and value-added processes, and analyze the functionalities of different types of information systems in an organization.
- **Strategic Acquisition:** Evaluate IT/system acquisition options, describe how to acquire resources and capabilities, and assess the performance of these investments.
- **Problem Mitigation:** Describe methods to mitigate issues related to information systems usage, operation, and development.

# Specific Pedagogical Objectives by Topic

The lesson is structured into five key areas, each with its own set of goals:

1. **Foundational Concepts:**
    - Distinguish between **data and information** and understand the characteristics of high-quality information.
    - Identify the basic functions and components of a system and explore related career paths.
2. **Organizational Integration:**
    - Analyze the relationship between **organizations and information technology**.
    - Understand the **value chain** and how organizational structure, culture, and values influence IS implementation and competitive advantage.
3. **Business System Diversity:**
    - Categorize systems from both a **functional perspective** (e.g., Sales, HR, Finance) and a **constituency perspective** (e.g., TPS, MIS, DSS, ESS).
    - Illustrate functionalities of **enterprise applications** such as ERP, SCM, and CRM systems.
4. **Infrastructure and Performance:**
    - Define technical and human IT infrastructure and evaluate modern solutions like **Cloud Computing, IoT, and Edge Computing**.
    - Master the acquisition process, including **sourcing, outsourcing, and co-sourcing** strategies.
    - Learn to evaluate system effectiveness using factors like **usability, functionality, and reliability**.
5. **Social and Ethical Responsibility:**
    - Address issues like **e-waste**, computer-related crimes (e.g., hacking, phishing), and security management.
    - Examine the **moral dimensions** of the information age, including privacy, ethics, and **ergonomics** for employee health and safety
