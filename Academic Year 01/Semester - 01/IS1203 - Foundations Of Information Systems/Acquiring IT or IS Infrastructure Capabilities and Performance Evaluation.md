# 1. Defining IT Infrastructure

IT Infrastructure is the set of hardware and software required to operate an entire enterprise. It is categorized into two main types:

- **Technical IT Infrastructure:** Includes the technology, applications, and datasets.
- **Human IT Infrastructure:** Includes data workers, information management personnel, and the knowledge and capabilities required to manage organizational IT resources.

# 2. Modern Hardware and Software Solutions

Organizations use various strategies and technologies to manage their infrastructure costs and capabilities:

- **Software Management:**
    - **Open-Source Applications:** Software where the source code can be modified and customized (e.g., Linux, Apache, Firefox).
    - **Integration Tools:** Technologies like **APIs**, **Middleware**, and **Cloud Integration** (e.g., Zapier) that allow different software products to interoperate and improve data flow.
- **Hardware & Computing Models:**
    - **On-Demand (Utility) Computing:** Resources are rented from an external provider and allocated based on need on a pay-per-use basis.
    - **Grid Computing:** Uses a network of computers to solve large-scale problems that are beyond the capacity of a single supercomputer.
    - **Edge Computing:** Addresses bandwidth and processing speed issues by moving computing closer to the data source.
    - **Autonomic Computing:** Systems designed to be self-configuring, self-optimizing, self-healing, and self-protecting.
    - **Green Computing:** Practices aimed at reducing power consumption and minimizing the environmental impact of IT through better design, use, and disposal.

# 3. Cloud Computing and IoT

- **Cloud Computing:** On-demand access to computing resources via the internet.
    - **IaaS (Infrastructure as a Service):** Renting raw processing, storage, and networking.
    - **PaaS (Platform as a Service):** Tools for developing and managing applications.
    - **SaaS (Software as a Service):** Ready-to-use applications hosted by a provider.
- **Internet of Things (IoT):** Using sensors and trackers to monitor business processes in real-time, helping to improve customer experience and save money.
- **IP Convergence:** The transmission of voice and multimedia content over internet connections, such as **VoIP** (e.g., Skype, Zoom).

# 4. Data and Knowledge Infrastructure

- **Operational Systems:** Perform **Online Transaction Processing (OLTP)** to run the business in real-time.
- **Informational Systems:** Support decision-making via **Online Analytical Processing (OLAP)** based on historical data.
- **Data Storage:** Includes **Data Lakes** (raw data), **Data Warehouses** (structured/organized data), and **Data Marts** (subject-oriented summarized data).

# 5. The IS Acquisition Process

Acquiring new systems involves five major steps: (1) Project Identification/Planning, (2) IT Architecture design, (3) Selection of Acquisition Options, (4) Testing/Integration, and (5) Operations/Maintenance.

**Major Acquisition Strategies:**

- **Build (In-sourcing):** Developing custom software in-house.
    - _Pros:_ Best fit for unique needs, full control over features.
    - _Cons:_ High cost, time-consuming, requires more IT personnel.
- **Buy (Off-the-Shelf):** Purchasing existing applications.
    - _Pros:_ Saves time, lower price, vendor-managed updates.
    - _Cons:_ May not meet all needs, difficult to modify.
    - _Note:_ Can be a **License** (one-time payment, lifetime use) or a **Subscription** (recurring payment, always up-to-date).
- **Outsourcing:** Contracting external suppliers to perform IS activities.
- **Co-sourcing:** A partnership where a firm acquires specialized services from a provider while keeping functional control in-house.
- **Offshoring:** Acquiring services or resources from another country to take advantage of lower costs or tax relief.

# 6. Evaluating IS Performance

An effective IT infrastructure must be **Flexible** (offering connectivity and modularity) and **Robust** (reliable, secure, and accurate).

**Factors used to assess system performance:**

1. **Usability:** The quality of the user experience.
2. **Functionality:** The ability of the product to perform its intended work.
3. **Reliability:** Performing without errors or interruptions.
4. **Performance:** Measured by accuracy and speed.
5. **Security:** Protection of sensitive data.
6. **Agility:** The ability to respond to new opportunities or pressures to change.
