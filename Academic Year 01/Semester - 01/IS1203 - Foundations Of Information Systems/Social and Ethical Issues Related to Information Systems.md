# 1. Computer-Related Waste and Mistakes

- **Definition of e-Waste:** This refers to inappropriate use of computer technology and resources in both public and private sectors, including discarded technology and unused systems.
- **Toxic Components:** Many older devices contain hazardous materials like **lead, arsenic, mercury, and chromium**.
- **Waste Management Hierarchy:** Organizations should follow a prioritized approach to waste: **Prevent** (better design) → **Reduce** (sustainable consumption) → **Reuse** (repair/refurbish) → **Recycle** (dismantle for materials) → **Recover** (energy) → **Dispose** (safely).
- **Prevention Strategies:** To eliminate waste and mistakes, firms should establish clear policies for acquisition and disposal, implement training for employees, and perform **internal audits** to monitor compliance.

# 2. Computer Crimes and Malware

- **Major Types of Crime:**
    - **Identity Theft:** An imposter obtains personal information to impersonate someone else.
    - **Cyberterrorism:** Computer-based attacks launched against systems or networks to advance political objectives.
    - **Illegal Access:** This includes **Hackers** (who study systems to find holes), **Crackers** (who gain unauthorized access), and **Malicious Insiders** (employees who harm systems from within).
- **Malicious Software (Malware):**
    - **Virus:** Malicious programs that attach to files and spread when executed.
    - **Worm:** Independent programs that replicate across networks without needing human action.
    - **Trojan Horse:** Malicious software disguised as legitimate programs to trick users.
    - **Ransomware:** Encrypts victim files and demands payment for the decryption key.
- **Social Engineering:** Includes **Phishing** (deceptive emails/SMS to steal credentials), **Vishing** (voice-based), and **Spear Phishing** (targeted at specific individuals).

# 3. Security Management and Defenses

- **Encryption:** The process of using mathematical algorithms (keys) to encode data before transmission, ensuring it can only be read by authorized recipients.
- **Firewalls:** Act as a "gatekeeper" system that filters traffic to protect internal networks from unauthorized internet users.
- **Biometrics:** Using human characteristics (physiological or behavioral) for identification and security.
- **Intrusion Detection:** Software that monitors networks for suspicious activity and notifies security personnel.
- **Data Protection Steps:** Organizations should restrict physical access to servers, use **audit trails** to trace transactions, and ensure all software is updated with the latest security patches.

# 4. Privacy and Ethical Issues

- **Privacy:** The claim of individuals to be left alone and free from surveillance. In IS, this involves concerns over the collection and misuse of personal data.
- **Ethics:** Principles of right and wrong used by individuals to guide their behavior. Key concepts include:
    - **Responsibility:** Accepting the costs and obligations of your decisions.
    - **Accountability:** Mechanisms to determine who took a specific action.
    - **Liability:** Laws that allow individuals to recover damages done to them by others.
- **Technology Trends Affecting Ethics:** Rising ethical issues are driven by doubling computing power, declining storage costs (making massive databases easier to maintain), and advances in data analysis (creating detailed profiles of individuals).

# 5. Ergonomics and Workplace Health

- **Ergonomics:** The study of designing and positioning computer equipment to ensure employee health and safety.
- **Health Concerns:**
    - **Repetitive Strain Injury (RSI):** Pain in muscles or nerves caused by repetitive movement.
    - **Carpal Tunnel Syndrome (CTS):** Irritation of wrist tendons causing numbness.
    - **Computer Vision Syndrome (CVS):** Eye irritation, blurred vision, and headaches from prolonged screen use.
    - **Techno-Stress:** Stress caused by technology use; it can be managed by setting boundaries, taking breaks, and practicing mindfulness.
- **Prevention:** Maintain good posture, use stretching exercises, and ensure screens are positioned 0-20 degrees below the horizontal line of sight.
