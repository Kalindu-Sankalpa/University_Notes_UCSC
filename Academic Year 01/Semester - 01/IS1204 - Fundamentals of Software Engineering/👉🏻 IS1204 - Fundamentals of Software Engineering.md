> **IS1204: Fundamentals of Software Engineering**, the overarching objective of this lesson is to equip university students with a systematic and disciplined approach to the development and maintenance of quality software products.

# The goals of this course can be categorized into three primary levels:

## 1. Overall Subject Objectives

By the end of this course, students are expected to reach a professional level of competence where they can:

- **Define and understand** software engineering principles, concepts, and why they are vital to modern society.
- **Evaluate different software processes** and techniques to ensure the systematic development of high-quality software.
- **Apply these principles** appropriately to design and implement software systems of moderate complexity.

## 2. Specific Competency Goals (The Software Lifecycle)

The lesson is structured to guide students through every phase of the software development lifecycle (SDLC), with specific learning goals for each:

- **Process Modeling:** Appreciate the need for defined processes and be able to compare and choose between various models, such as plan-driven versus agile approaches.
- **Agile Development:** Understand the rationale behind the **Agile Manifesto**, its core values, and practices like iterative delivery and customer collaboration.
- **Requirements Engineering:** Learn the process of gathering, defining, and validating what a system should do, including distinguishing between functional and non-functional requirements.
- **System Modeling:** Understand how to use graphical models, specifically **UML diagrams** (like class and sequence diagrams), to represent and document software systems from different perspectives.
- **Architectural Design:** Comprehend how software systems should be organized using common architectural styles and patterns.
- **Design & Implementation:** Master object-oriented design (OOD) concepts—such as **abstraction, inheritance, polymorphism, and encapsulation**—and understand how to realize these designs through executable programs.
- **Software Testing:** Learn the stages of the testing process to discover program defects and demonstrate that a system meets its intended requirements.
- **Software Evolution:** Recognize that software change is inevitable and understand the processes for maintenance, re-engineering, and **configuration management** to keep systems useful over time.

## 3. Professional and Ethical Goals

Beyond technical skills, the lesson aims to foster professional responsibility in students by:

- Recognizing the **ethical and professional issues** that are critical for software engineers.
- Understanding the **key challenges** facing the field, such as software costs and the risks associated with "software disasters".
- Promoting **sustainable development** and technical excellence as defined by modern industry standards.

In summary, the goal of this lesson is to transform a student from a "programmer" into a **"software engineer"** who can manage the complexity, quality, and evolution of professional software systems

# Topics Covered

**IS1204: Fundamentals of Software Engineering** cover a broad range of topics organized into nine major chapters that follow the software development lifecycle.

1. [[Introduction to Software Engineering]]
	- **Definitions and Basics:** What software is, types of software, and the characteristics of software.
	- **The Field of Software Engineering:** Definition of software engineering, its relationship with systems engineering, and associated costs.
	- **Key Challenges:** Understanding current industry challenges and the history of "software disasters".
	- **Ethics:** Professional and ethical issues critical for software engineers.

2. [[Software Process Models]]
	- **Process Fundamentals:** The need for defined processes and the basic activities of specification, design, validation, and evolution.
	- **Plan-Driven Models:**
	    - Waterfall Model
	    - Incremental Development
	    - Reuse-Oriented Software Engineering
	    - Iterative Development
	    - Spiral and Prototyping Models
	- **Choosing a Model:** Comparing different models and selecting the right one based on project type.

3. [[Agile Software Development]]
	- **Agile Foundation:** The rationale for agile methods, the **Agile Manifesto** (core values), and the **12 Agile Principles**.
	- **Agile Life Cycle:** Working in iterations or "sprints," and central activities like requirement gathering and design.
	- **Specific Frameworks:** Scrum, Extreme Programming (XP), Kanban, Lean, and Feature Driven Development.
	- **Agile vs. Traditional:** Comparing plan-driven approaches with agile flexibility

4. [[Requirements Engineering]]
	- **Requirement Types:** Distinguishing between functional and non-functional requirements, and user vs. system requirements.
	- **The RE Process:** Elicitation, analysis, specification, validation, and managing requirement changes.
	- **Agile Requirements:** The use of **user stories** and incremental requirements engineering.

5. [[System Modeling]]
	- **Modeling Perspectives:** Using context, interaction, structural, and behavioral models to view a system from different angles.
	- **Unified Modeling Language (UML):** The standard language for object-oriented modeling
	- **Diagram Types:**
	    - Use Case diagrams
	    - Sequence diagrams
	    - Class diagrams
	    - Activity and State diagrams

6. [[Architectural Design]]
	- **Fundamental Organization:** Defining software architecture and basic principles like abstraction, encapsulation, and modularity.
	- **Design Decisions:** Creative decisions regarding application types and system distribution.
	- **Architectural Views:** The "4+1" view model, including logical, process, development, and physical views.
	- **Patterns:** Commonly used architectural patterns such as **Model-View-Controller (MVC)**, layered architecture, repository, client-server, and pipe-and-filter.

7. [[Design and Implementation]]
	- **Object-Oriented Design (OOD):** Using UML to identify classes, attributes, and behaviors (specialization/generalization).
	- **Design Patterns:** Understanding creational, structural, and behavioral patterns (e.g., the Observer pattern).
	- **Implementation Issues:** Software reuse levels, **configuration management**, and host-target management.
	- **Open Source:** Open source development, licensing, and license management.

8. [[Software Testing]]
	- **Testing Stages:** Development testing, release testing, and user testing.
	- **Methods:** **Test-driven development (TDD)** and test-first development.
	- **Techniques:** Selecting test cases to discover defects and validating that a system meets requirements.

9. [[Software Evolution]]
	- **Evolution Processes:** Change processes for operational systems and program evolution dynamics.
	- **Maintenance:** Different types of software maintenance and the maintenance process.
	- **Configuration Management:** Establishing standards and procedures for managing evolving software products across different versions.
