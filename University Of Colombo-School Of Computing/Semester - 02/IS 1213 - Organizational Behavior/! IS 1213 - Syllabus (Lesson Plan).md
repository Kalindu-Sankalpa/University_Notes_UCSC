#Lesson-Plan 

>[!Goal]
>This course, **Organizational Behavior**, is designed to explore how individuals and groups behave within organizational settings and how these behaviors impact the overall success of the organization. Drawing from fields like psychology, sociology, and anthropology, the course aims to provide you with the tools to understand, motivate, and manage teams effectively, which is critical for fostering inclusive, innovative, and productive work environments.

# The learning objectives are categorized by major topics as follows:

## Foundations of Organizational Behavior and Diversity

1. **Describe the two major forms of workforce diversity** (surface-level and deep-level).
2. Define key **biographical characteristics** (such as age, gender, and race) and describe their relevance to the workplace.
3. Define **intellectual ability** and demonstrate its relevance to organizational behavior, while contrasting the two types of ability (intellectual and physical).
4. Describe how organizations can **manage diversity effectively**.
5. Explain how **culture** affects our understanding of biographical characteristics and intellectual abilities.

## Personality and Values

1. Define **personality**, describe how it is measured, and explain the factors that determine it.
2. Describe and assess personality frameworks, specifically the **Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI)** and the **Big Five Model**.
3. Demonstrate how Big Five traits **predict behavior at work**.
4. Define **values**, explain their importance, and contrast different types of values.
5. Identify generational differences in values and **Hofstede’s six value dimensions** of national culture.

## Perception and Individual Decision Making

1. Define **perception** and explain the factors that influence it.
2. Explain **attribution theory** and identify the shortcuts individuals use when making judgments about others (such as stereotyping or the halo effect).
3. Explain the link between **perception and decision making**.
4. Apply the **rational model of decision making** and contrast it with bounded rationality and intuition.
5. List and explain common **decision biases or errors** (such as overconfidence or anchoring bias).
6. Explain how individual differences and organizational constraints affect decision making.
7. Contrast the three **ethical decision criteria** (utilitarianism, rights, and justice).
8. Define **creativity** and discuss the three-component model of creativity.

## Motivation, Leadership, and Group Dynamics

1. Understand **motivation** as the driving force behind employee productivity and explore theories such as Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs and Herzberg’s Two-Factor Theory.
2. Understand the role of **leadership** in influencing others, building trust, and fostering innovation.
3. Analyze **group behavior and work teams** to improve decision-making and productivity.
4. Recognize the importance of **effective communication** in collaboration and conflict resolution.
5. Identify the impact of **emotions and moods** on the workplace and organizational culture.
6. Define **organizational culture** and understand its importance for employee retention and productivity.
